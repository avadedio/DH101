## Reflection

Making this project counts as the studying and recognition of patterns from the data collected. Firstly, the process of filtering and navigating what faces are selected and included in the dataset counts as making in this project, and it begs the question of how the model ensures diversity and "humanness" when continuing on with a certain dataset of faces. The model design also counts as intentional making; what it is trained to prioritize matters, such as specificity of features versus diversity, or realism versus accuracy, and other measures and considerations. Making in the output includes the pattern or lack thereof of faces that are shown, as well as how often they are refreshed and updated by the model. The interface design is also an intentional aspect of making in this project - selecting the design of showing just one image at a time counts as making. The fact that there are no words and a straight on, a lack of contextual or distracting backgrounds, and face only camera shots are also intentional in the making process. When machines participate in making, the intent shifts - humans can no longer control the output totally, but they decide the range and aesthetic possibilities of what can be automated by the machine participation. While machine participation extends the possibilities of output, it also obscures responsibility, making it harder to hold accountability for bias and other issues of representation in the output.
